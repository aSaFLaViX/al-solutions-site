Case study

From ad-hoc data requests to a self-service Data Copilot

A mid-size data-driven organization wanted to free its analysts from repetitive ad-hoc requests and give business teams direct, governed access to insights.

Turning questions into governed, explainable answers

Client ¬∑ Mid-size data-driven company ¬∑ Multiple SQL / ES|QL / BigQuery sources ¬∑ Central data team

Challenge

Analysts were drowning in ad-hoc questions from product, research and operations. Simple requests could take days due to queue and context switching, while most business users could not write SQL or navigate the schema. Metric definitions lived in scattered documents and ‚Äútribal knowledge‚Äù.

Approach

We designed and implemented a Data Copilot that:

  • Understands the data schema, relationships and constraints
  • Reads internal documentation via RAG (metric definitions, methodologies, caveats)
  • Translates natural language into SQL / ES|QL / BigQuery
  • Executes queries through a controlled service layer that respects existing roles and views
  • Returns both numeric results and business explanations, with links to underlying docs

How it works

The Copilot pipeline looks like this:

  • 1 ¬∑ Understand the question ‚Äì intent, metrics, dimensions, filters, time windows.
  • 2 ¬∑ Plan ‚Äì identify which tables/views and which engine (SQL / ES|QL / BigQuery) are needed.
  • 3 ¬∑ Generate queries ‚Äì produce and validate queries against the schema and business rules.
  • 4 ¬∑ Execute safely ‚Äì run through a service that enforces existing permissions.
  • 5 ¬∑ Enrich with docs ‚Äì fetch relevant metric definitions and caveats via RAG.
  • 6 ¬∑ Answer ‚Äì return tables/aggregations plus a short written explanation and links to sources.

Exact numbers vary by client, but typical outcomes include:

  • [X‚ÄìY% reduction] in analyst time spent on repeated ad-hoc queries
  • Time from question to answer down from [N days] to [M minutes/hours]
  • Higher adoption of official metric definitions across teams

3-week pilot structure

  • Week 1 ¬∑ Discovery & mapping
    Identify core use-cases and user groups, map relevant schemas, and collect existing metric definitions and documentation.
  • Week 2 ¬∑ Knowledge layer & prototype
    Build a combined schema + docs knowledge layer, configure planning/query agents, and connect to staging datasets.
  • Week 3 ¬∑ Pilot & refinement
    Run the Copilot with a small group of users, review generated queries, refine guardrails and prompts, and prepare rollout plan.
Security & governance
Queries never bypass your access controls. The model is used as a stateless reasoning engine; it does not get raw, unrestricted access to your databases. All questions and generated queries are logged for audit and tuning.
